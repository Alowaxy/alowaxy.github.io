Inverse Kinematics Solver using Supervised Learning (PyTorch) - Personal Project

Learned fundamental machine learning and supervised learning concepts through the development of an inverse kinematics solver for a 2 degree of freedom robot arm.

Overview: This project was a hands-on exploration of machine learning concepts using PyTorch. Using object-oriented programming in python, I initially created a forward kinematics solver, manually generated joint angle data, and used it as input for training a simple neural network model. The model was built using PyTorch and was trained with supervised learning to predict joint angles from end effector positions. I designed the neural network with nn.Linear layers and used the ReLU activation function. For optimization, I employed the Adam optimizer and used mean squared error loss as the criterion. With 1200 points of data and 1200 training loops, the model achieved decent accuracy with an average error of roughly 4.64%. The model accuracy can be improved by using a larger data set, more training loops and using more layers with double the amount of neurons.

2-DOF Humanoid Wrist Mechanism Prototype

Designed, modeled and fabricated a 2-degree-of-freedom wrist prototype using SolidWorks for the Humanoid Robot Club. Handled the CAD assembly and the integration of 3D-printed components.

Overview: I engineered this mechanism to enable complex hand movements while ensure that it remains lightweight, minimizing the moment arm to reduce power consumption on the shoulder motors.

Design Implementation: To prevent back-driving without constant power draw, I utilized two small linear actuators with internal locking mechanisms rather than pneumatics, hydraulics or even rotary motors coupled with a slider-crank linkage. This configuration, paired with spherical joints, provides active control over yaw and pitch.

Technical Challenges: The initial design used a central spherical joint to mount the end effector, which inadvertently introduced a passive roll degree of freedom. I fixed this by designing a custom yaw-pitch connection piece in SolidWorks to eliminate the passive rotation, ensuring the end effector remains stable under external loads.

1-DOF Pure Rigid Body Robot Finger Mechanism Design

Designed a novel 1-DOF self-curling robotic finger mechanism comprised of 7 rigid body links.

Overview: I wanted to design a finger mechanism with 1 active DOF that automatically closed while using only rigid body linkages.

Design Implementation: I started by sketching out multiple links, visualizing the motion, and then adjusting the arrangement until I believed I had achieved the desired motion. I then modeled the links in SolidWorks, constrained them and in assembly, tested the motion, and fine-tuned geometry of each link.

Handheld Coaxial Contra-Rotating Fan

Currently designing a handheld contra-rotating fan powered by a single motor. Designed and modeled a custom bevel gear transmission system in SolidWorks to achieve synchronized counter-rotation.

Overview: Coaxial contra-rotating propelers are used on aircrafts partly because they provide more direct linear thrust. Taking inspiration, I wanted to design and fabricate a handheld coaxial contra-rotating fan.

Design Implementation: I first modeled the fan blades and the bevel gears in SolidWorks. I also created an assembly and attached an 8mm shaft to the fan blades and bevel gears. Ball-bearings were added to reduce friction and set-screws were implemented. The 2 set screws are placed 180° apart diametrically, keeping the moment of inertia symmetric, which helps maintain balance and minimize vibrations. The motor was added to the assembly and the fan housing was modeled to cater to the existing spacing and geometry of the moving parts. I am currently designing the fan's electrical system.

Technical Challenges: I anticipate issues with friction, vibration, heat, and fit.
